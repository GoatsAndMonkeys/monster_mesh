🧬 COLOR VARIANTS — ODDS & GENETICS

MonsterMesh Pokémon come in 12 color skins + a hidden Shiny Carrier (13 genotypes). Shiny stays game-authentic (1/4096);
the rest is derived from three genes.

🎨 THE 12 SKINS  (4 colors × 3 dark-dosage levels)

          Normal    Dark(Dd)       Blackout(DD)
Regular   Regular   Dark           Blackout
Shiny     Shiny     Dark-Shiny     Blackout-Shiny
Pink      Pink      Dark-Pink      Blackout-Pink
Rainbow   Rainbow   Dark-Rainbow   Blackout-Rainbow

Dark = greyscale body, accent kept. Blackout = black "Who's-That-Pokémon" silhouette. Rainbow = animated diagonal rainbow.

🔬 THE THREE GENES

Gene     Alleles          Mode
Rainbow  R/r (X-linked)   incomplete dominance, ♀-ONLY
Shiny    S/s              recessive (hidden carrier)
Dark     D/d              dosage (Dd=Dark, DD=Blackout)

Rainbow - Rr shows as Pink, rr = Rainbow 🌈
Shiny   - ss = Shiny; Ss = hidden carrier (~1/32)
Dark    - Dd = Dark, DD = Blackout 🖤

🐈 PINK & RAINBOW ARE CALICO — FEMALES ONLY

The Rainbow gene sits on the X chromosome, so only females can show Pink or Rainbow:
- Males can only CARRY the color, never show it — every son comes out Regular-looking.
- Only daughters can be Pink or Rainbow.
- Since ~half a clutch is male, at most half the clutch can be Pink/Rainbow.

It's criss-cross inheritance: a Rainbow mom's r hides in her sons and resurfaces as Pink/Rainbow in her granddaughters.
A Rainbow father is impossible — the r always rides in on an invisible male carrier, so you must blood-test your studs. 🩸

🎲 WILD ODDS (population-wide)

Pink ♀            1 in 1,024
Shiny             1 in 4,096
Dark              1 in 4,096
Rainbow ♀         1 in 2.1 million
Dark-Pink ♀       1 in 4.2 million
Dark-Shiny        1 in 16.8 million
Blackout          1 in 67 million
Dark-Rainbow ♀    1 in ~8.6 billion
Blackout-Rainbow  1 in ~140 trillion  👑

♀ = females only (≈2× that rate among females). Rainbow and Blackout are so rare you breed for them, not find them.

In Pentest Pikachu the color is a deterministic hash of the network's MAC + species — a given AP always shows the same
color for the same mon.
```

🥚 BREEDING

Bred mons ship a full genetic report automatically; you blood-test wild catches yourself.

Ss × Ss              carriers → 25% Shiny
Dd × Dd              → 50% Dark · 25% Blackout
Rainbow♀ × carrier♂  → 100% of DAUGHTERS Rainbow

Rainbow needs a Pink/Rainbow mother AND a carrier father. Blackout-Rainbow = stack a Rainbow line + a Blackout line —
the rarest genotype in the game.

⚠️ HIDDEN DEFECTS (this is what makes breeding hard)

Every mon secretly carries recessive defect genes:

Defect       Gene  xx effect          Alive?
Sterile      B/b   can't breed          ✅
Can't-fight  F/f   can't battle         ✅
No-hatch     H/h   egg never hatches    ❌
*ff can still breed unless also bb.

What shows up wild (an encounter is a battle):
- hh → never alive → never wild
- ff → never in an encounter → can't catch one wild
- bb sterile → fights fine → CAN appear wild (~1/16)

💔 Catch a Wild Rainbow, blood-test it → bb sterile. Battle-ready and gorgeous, but a genetic dead-end.

🏷️ PROVENANCE TAG

Wild     caught, not bred — pure luck
F1,F2..  filial (Rainbow first appears F2)
S1,S2..  selfing (toward true-breeding)
BX1..n   backcross (BX4 ≈ 97% one line)
IBL      true-breeding line
```

🏆 WHAT MAKES A MON VALUABLE

A "fancy" mon is any with a cosmetic (Shiny, Pink, Rainbow, Dark, Blackout). But cosmetic rarity alone is the LOWEST
rung — a fancy mon that can't fight or breed is an ORNAMENTAL: a fancy goldfish, bred for looks, none of the hardiness.
Real value = viability.

Ornamental   rare skin but bb/ff → collectible only
Clean stock  BB FF HH → prime breeding foundation
Workhorse    IBL line that breeds a rare true
Crown jewel  rare AND viable — a fertile, battle-ready mon

🔍 BREEDERS vs BUYERS — WHO WANTS WHAT

Value splits across axes that pull against each other. Where you sit depends on whether you're a BREEDER building a program
or a CUSTOMER who wants one good mon right now.

A BREEDER thinks in LINES, not individuals:
- Clean genetics. Blood-test everything; pair mons that DON'T share a defect, or you homozygose it.
- Fertility above all. A sterile (bb) rare is a dead-end, however gorgeous.
- True-breeding rares (IBL). Lock a rare color homozygous so it breeds 100% true — AND clean of every defect.
- Carriers as tools. Rainbow is X-linked, so breeders hunt hidden carrier STUDS to funnel the color into daughters. Your
  ugliest plain catch may be your best foundation stock.

A CUSTOMER isn't running a program — they want one great mon:
- Battle-grade. Can actually fight, good stats. For most players this is the whole point.
- A fancy look to flex. Any cosmetic — Shiny, Pink, Rainbow, Dark, Blackout.
- Provenance / the story. A WILD Rainbow (luck) vs a BX4 Rainbow (engineered) look identical but flex differently.
- Ornamentals. A fancy mon that can't fight or breed is fun to show off, but the lowest prestige rung alone.

👑 THE OVERLAP = THE TROPHIES
The crown jewels are fancy AND viable — a fertile, battle-ready IBL Rainbow is the whole package. Breeders build them;
customers pay for them.

TL;DR: breeders buy potential, customers buy performance.
```
